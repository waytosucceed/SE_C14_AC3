Division with Decimals

Division with decimals is a fundamental mathematical operation used in various contexts from academic subjects to everyday financial calculations. This content will focus on two specific types of division involving decimals: dividing a decimal number by a whole number and dividing a decimal number by another decimal number.

Dividing a Decimal Number by a Whole Number

  1. Direct Division: Divide the decimal number by the whole number just as you would with any basic division operation.
  2. Place the Decimal Point: Ensure the decimal point in the quotient is correctly placed directly above its position in the dividend.

Example:

  • Problem: Divide 12.56 by 4.
    • Division: Perform the division as normal, 12.56 ÷ 4.
    • Calculating: 12.56 divided by 4 equals 3.14.

Result: 12.56 ÷ 4 = 3.14, where the quotient, 3.14, reflects the division of each part of the decimal evenly by 4.


Division with Decimals

1. Division of a Decimal Number by a Whole Number

When dividing a decimal by a whole number, convert the decimal into its fractional equivalent and then perform the division.

Example:

  • Divide 0.75 by 3
  • Convert 0.75 to a fraction: 0.75 = 75100

  • Simplify the fraction: 75100 = 75 ÷ 25100 ÷ 25 = 34

  • Perform the division: 34 ÷ 3 = 34 × 13 = 312 = 14

  • Convert back to decimal if needed: 14 = 0.25

Result: 0.75 divided by 3 equals 0.25.

2. Division of a Whole Number by a Decimal Number

To divide a whole number by a decimal, convert both numbers into fractions and then multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor.

Example:

  • Divide 5 by 0.2
  • Convert 5 and 0.2 into fractions: 5 = 51 and 0.2 = 210 = 15

  • Solve : 5 ÷ 0.2 = 51 ÷ 15 = 51 × 51 = 251 = 25

Result: 5 divided by 0.2 equals 25.

3. Division of a Decimal Number by Another Decimal Number

Dividing one decimal by another involves converting both decimals to fractions, simplifying, and using the reciprocal of the divisor.

Example:

  • Divide 1.2 by 0.4
  • Convert to fractions:1.2 = 1210 = 65 and 0.4 = 410 = 25

  • Dividing : 1.2 ÷ 0.4 = 65 ÷ 25 = 65 × 52 = 306 = 5

Result: 1.2 divided by 0.4 equals 5.
